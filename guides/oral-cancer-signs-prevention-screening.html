Frequently Asked Questions

What are the risk factors for oral cancer?

Risk factors include tobacco use, alcohol consumption, HPV infection, sun exposure (lip cancer), and family history.

What are early signs of oral cancer?

Watch for persistent sores, lumps, color changes, difficulty swallowing, voice changes, and unexplained mouth bleeding.

How is oral cancer screened?

Dentists perform visual and physical examinations during regular checkups. Specialized tests may be needed for suspicious areas.

Can oral cancer be prevented?

Reduce risk by quitting tobacco and alcohol, limiting sun exposure, maintaining oral hygiene, and getting HPV vaccination.

What is the survival rate for oral cancer?

Early-stage survival rates exceed 80%. Late-stage cancers have lower survival rates, making early detection critical.
