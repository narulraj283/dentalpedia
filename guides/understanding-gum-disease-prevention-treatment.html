Frequently Asked Questions

What causes gum disease?

Gum disease is primarily caused by plaque and tartar buildup, poor oral hygiene, smoking, and genetic predisposition.

Is gum disease reversible?

Gingivitis (early stage) is reversible with improved oral hygiene. Periodontitis (advanced) requires professional treatment but can be managed.

How is gum disease treated?

Treatment ranges from improved oral hygiene to scaling and root planing, antibiotics, or gum surgery depending on severity.

Can gum disease cause tooth loss?

Yes, untreated periodontitis is the leading cause of tooth loss in adults. Early treatment is essential.

How can I prevent gum disease?

Brush twice daily, floss daily, avoid smoking, maintain a healthy diet, and visit your dentist regularly.
